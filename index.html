About Me

I am an AI researcher interested in simulation-based learning, large language models, reinforcement learning, and agent AI. My recent work focuses on predicting fall-from-bed risks using physics-based simulation and skeleton-based machine learning models.

More broadly, I am interested in building practical AI systems that connect simulation, decision-making, and real-world healthcare applications.

Selected Projects

Fall-from-Bed Risk Prediction

Physics-based simulation / Skeleton-based ML / Healthcare AI

Developed a physics-based simulation framework for predicting fall risk from static human posture on a bed. Evaluated multiple models including RF, CNN, and GCN to analyze how much predictive signal can be extracted from initial posture alone.

Skeleton-based Risk Modeling

Graph representation / Coordinate-based learning

Investigated machine learning models trained on joint coordinate representations to estimate fall risk. Focused on representation quality, generalization, and the limitations of static pose-based prediction.

LLM-based Healthcare Documentation Support

LLM / Structured event generation / Clinical workflow

Explored the use of large language models for converting structured patient-state information into natural clinical log-style text, with an emphasis on reliability, validation, and safe generation.
